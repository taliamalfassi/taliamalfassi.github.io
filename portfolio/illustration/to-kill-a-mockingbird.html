To Kill a Mockingbird

ILLUSTRATION EDITORIAL

A book by Harper Lee
During the Great Depression, in a small-town Alabama, the lawyer Atticus Finch is the public defender of an Afro-American accused of raping a white woman. The lawyer succeeds in proving his innocence, but the black man will be sentenced to death anyway.
DATE

April 2020

ROLE

Digital Illustrator

COMPANY

Personal Project

APPROACH

I decided to illustrate the scene from the main character's eyes, the young Scout, Atticus's daughter, in the company of her older brother and their best friend, as it happens in the novel. This illustration is a glimpse of the life in Maycomb portrayed from Scout's point of view and at the same time from the reader's point of view. The style used would simulate the engraving technique. Only a stroke so decisive can symbolize an intense story like that.
